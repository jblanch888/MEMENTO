# Contributing

This framework grows on deployment evidence. The most valuable contribution is a field report: you ran some part of Memento on real work, and something held, failed, or surprised you.

## What is welcome

- **Field reports.** Which rung of the graduation ladder, what you fitted, what earned its keep, what you killed and why. Receipts beat opinions; a dated incident beats a preference.
- **Fitting notes.** If you adapted a template to a host the lineage has not seen (a research group, a data platform, a solo product), your fitting note is portability evidence.
- **Corrections with evidence.** This canon binds its claims to receipts; if you can show a claim wrong, that correction is the discipline working, and it is wanted.
- **Improvements to templates and docs** that keep them honest, small, and adopter-fitted.

## House rules

- **British English** throughout, and the canon's writing rules apply to canon text.
- **Claims trace to evidence.** State what you observed, where, and how it can be checked. Honest UNVERIFIED labels are welcome, and every confident assertion needs its receipt.
- **No confidential material.** Name your own deployments generically if they are private; this canon does the same with its own.
- **Nothing is deleted from the record.** Superseded content is bannered and archived; the killed-mechanism roll only grows.

## Mechanics

Issues for questions, reports and corrections; pull requests from a feature branch (`type/short-description`) for changes, with commit messages in `type(scope): summary` form. Significant changes to governing templates or the story get discussed in an issue first: the story is receipted, so changing it means changing what the receipts support.
