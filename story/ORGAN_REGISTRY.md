# The Organ Registry

The mid-2026 framework is not a monolith. It is a set of **organs**: mechanisms and disciplines that can each be independently adopted, traced, or killed. This registry is the public form of a frozen, audited registry of thirty-two organs, traced across the lineage's three living estates with commit-level receipts. Verdicts were made first-hand by the auditing estate; scout-gathered evidence was never accepted without primary verification.

**How to read the table.** *Origin* is the era and estate where the organ first appears in the receipts (names generic by policy). *Spread* records how widely the organ took: **universal** organs appear in all three estates in some form; **host-specific** organs answer one estate's circumstances. Both kinds matter: the universal spine is what an adopter starts from, and the host-specific organs demonstrate that the framework adapts rather than photocopies.

## What the spread means

Roughly two dozen organs form a **universal spine**: present in every estate, in fittings sized to each host. The remainder are honest adaptations: a validation ledger where governance experiments concentrate, dual threads where framework and product share one worktree, blast-radius fencing where multiple people touch confidential material. An adopter should read the spread column as advice: the spine first, the specialised organs when your circumstances create the need. That sequencing is the graduation ladder, described in the adoption guidance (`adoption/GRADUATION_LADDER.md`, landing later in this rebuild).
