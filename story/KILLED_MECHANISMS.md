# The Killed-Mechanism Roll

This framework publishes its dead mechanisms alongside its living ones. The current epoch's defining rule requires it: **every mechanism carries a pre-registered way to prove it worthless, and killed mechanisms are documented rather than buried.** The roll below is the evidence that the framework's governance claims get tested, and it holds some of the most useful material in the canon: each death paid for a lesson.

Each entry names the mechanism, how it ended (killed on evidence, held and superseded, or declined before birth), and what the ending taught. Dates are from the lineage's evidence archives; estates are named generically by policy.

## 1. The commit-approval gate

A hook that asked the human to approve every commit. Over thirty days it fired 154 times and was approved 154 times. An ask that is always approved is friction wearing the costume of enforcement. **Killed July 2026.** The real protective layer was the deterministic pre-commit checks running beneath it all along. *Lesson: measure the refusal rate of any approval gate; one hundred per cent approval means the gate governs nothing.*

## 2. The stranded charter injector

A mechanism meant to inject a governance charter into sessions. It ran in shadow mode behind an environment flag that was never set, anywhere, at any time: roughly 450 shadow firings, zero live injections, across its entire lifetime. Nothing noticed, because nothing was watching for its absence. Found by a deep health audit and **removed July 2026.** *Lesson: silence is not health. Every mechanism needs an unconditional witness that it actually runs, because a dead mechanism looks identical to a quiet one.*

## 3. The self-asserted restart protocol

The first restart protocol was a thoughtful design with three layers: a checklist ticked with quoted evidence, an anti-gaming clause requiring rules to be quoted back as proof they were loaded, and a synthesis gate asking the agent to state in its own words the mission, the constraints, the principles most relevant to the situation, and the next safe action with its rationale. **Retired as theatre, June 2026**, on hard evidence. The checklist was self-attested (the agent ticking the boxes was the agent making the claims). A root-cause analysis then showed an agent restating a rule in the very message where it breached it: reading a rule aloud does not prevent violating it, and the spike's verdict on the synthesis layer was that paraphrase is as free as a ticked box. The estate's own retrospective priced the protocol at roughly three-quarters performance, one-quarter load-bearing. The load-bearing quarter survived: live-state reconciliation, mechanised into a claims-versus-reality diff verified against files and git state, with mismatches dispositioned one by one, while the situational judgement itself stayed with the agent. *Lesson: even sophisticated reflection is a performance when the performer grades it. Keep what a machine can check against reality, keep the judgement, and drop the recital.*

## 4. The routing-enforcement hook

An attempt to mechanise the routing law by counting the main agent's tool calls at the end of each turn. It metered call counts when the discipline is about fit; it fired without enforcing; it was blind to half the failure modes it existed to catch. **Removed as net-negative, 2026.** The routing loop remains agent discipline, run because it is right. *Lesson: judgement-shaped rules resist mechanisation; a bad meter is worse than no meter, and some disciplines must stay prose until a mechanism can pass its own falsifier.*

## 5. The working-context edit gate

A hook checking that working-context edits were coherent replacements rather than appends. The rule matters (a real contamination incident created it), and the rule survives as directive discipline. The mechanical check did not: stale-content classification is content-aware in ways a hook cannot judge. **The sub-gate was cut, June 2026;** the dispatcher survived for checks a machine can actually make. *Lesson: keep the rule as discipline, and stop pretending a machine enforces it.*

## 6. The spend governor

A daily budget backstop for model spend, designed and then deliberately never built. Before it was wired, a structurally stronger mechanism arrived: a lifecycle in which failed items park in an errored state for human routing and never auto-retry, removing the runaway-spend path at its source. **Held, then superseded, July 2026.** *Lesson: prefer removing a failure path to metering it; the best mechanism is sometimes the one you decline to build.*

## 7. The cross-estate refusal

The clearest sign the lineage learns as one organism: a newly founded estate's backlog records a mechanical routing-enforcement hook as deliberately not imported, because a sibling estate had already disproved it. The death happened once; the lesson priced in everywhere. *Lesson: a killed mechanism's grave marker is portable, and honouring another estate's kill is cheaper than re-running the funeral.*

## 8. The orphaned parameters

A forensic audit of one estate's directives found a token-budget trigger configuring an automation that had been lost in a migration long before (the knob turned nothing), and a provenance label citing a protocol document that did not exist. Both **excised, June 2026**, and the directive re-annotated with an honest "value unverified on the current agent generation" status it still carries. *Lesson: directives rot like code; audit them forensically, and prefer an honest UNVERIFIED to a confident citation of nothing.*

## Running your own kill discipline

1. **Pre-register the falsifier when you install the mechanism.** State, in writing, the result that would prove it worthless, and when you will look.
2. **Witness unconditionally.** A mechanism with no execution log is indistinguishable from a dead one.
3. **Deliver verdicts on a clock, not on suspicion.** The ledger row's date arrives whether or not you feel like judging.
4. **Document the death.** The lesson is the durable asset. The mechanism was only ever its container.

The auditing estate that produced this canon runs under the same rule. Its first wired mechanism (a compaction gate) carries an "enforcement unverified" label until its first live test, because the rule applies most of all to the people writing it down.
