# The Story

This is the receipted history of the framework: where it was born, how it spread, what the dormant year hid, and why the current canon exists. Dates come from commit archaeology across the lineage's repositories; the audit memos behind every claim are held in the framework's own evidence archive. Product and client names are kept generic by policy.

## 2025: born in a working repository

Memento began as survival tactics inside the repository of a team-capacity roadmapping tool, where long-running AI-assisted development kept losing coherence between sessions. In late June 2025 those tactics crystallised into a named structure with two arms: a memory prosthesis (tiered externalised memory) and operational protocols (directives, playbooks, and the human's sovereignty over "done"). The first commit of that structure describes it exactly as it still is: a memory-prosthesis and protocols architecture.

It spread the way it would keep spreading: by deliberate transplant. Within a fortnight a personal knowledge assistant received the framework by migration; within a month a client-programme knowledge assistant was born with the framework already installed. That third implementation matured fastest and went furthest: multiple people used it, and it steered roughly a year of a team's delivery work (the author's own account; its commit history runs well into 2026). When the author extracted a public, sanitised canon in early August 2025, it drew on all three production implementations. The extraction notes survive, dated the eve of the canon's first commit.

The August 2025 canon is preserved verbatim in [`archive/canon-2025/`](../archive/canon-2025/). It was honest about its era: a documentation-discipline framework, enforced by prose and habit, because nothing else existed to enforce with.

## The dormant months

Then the canon froze. For roughly nine months between its release and May 2026 it received almost nothing; its final act of that era was a one-line fix to its own epoch count. A reader of the public repository would have concluded the project was finished or abandoned.

The field concluded neither. The origin repository kept running the framework the entire time, and the client-programme assistant steered a team's work deep into those same months. What looks like dormancy from the canon's vantage point was an incubation period in private: the framework was accumulating exactly the kind of history its own thesis says must not be lost, including failures that would later become its most valuable teaching material.

## 2026: the mechanised wave

In May 2026 the origin repository began a modernisation that defines the current epoch. The tooling that Epoch 3 had lacked finally existed: harness hooks, gates, session telemetry. The first hook arrived in early May. By mid-June the estate had grown a validation ledger (every governance mechanism facing a pre-registered falsifier on a clock), a routing law (the strongest model carries judgement, cheaper tiers carry bounded work, deterministic code carries everything code can decide), governance telemetry with the rule that silence is never read as health, and a frontmatter spine generating navigation and governance maps mechanically.

Then the transplants resumed, faster and more deliberate than in 2025:

- **Late June 2026:** a multi-person workstream memory system was founded: the framework's first host whose *product* is itself shared memory for a team's business workstreams. (It was not the framework's first multi-person service; the client-programme assistant had been serving a team since 2025.) Its founding text contains the lineage's clearest statement of the adoption model: begin with the framework's spirit, and earn the apparatus when the work's own weight demands it. What the record shows, honestly stated: the graduation-path text and the full apparatus entered that repository together in its first commit, with the text recording (in the author's own dating, made days earlier) the decision that the apparatus would be earned. Prediction and fulfilment arrived hand in hand, and the founding text explains why.
- **Early July 2026:** the planning discipline was rewritten in the origin repository from an empirical taxonomy of working sessions (the planning law's own provenance records ninety-four of them), and two days later an organisational cartography instrument was founded with the modern framework installed from its first commit. There the review discipline crystallised into a standing rule: consequential artefacts face an independent adversarial review, briefed to refute, before being presented at all.
- **Mid July 2026:** a deep health audit of the origin estate found, among much that was healthy, a governance mechanism that had been dead its entire life while appearing to run. The finding, and the rule it graduated ("silence is not health"), are recorded in [`KILLED_MECHANISMS.md`](KILLED_MECHANISMS.md).

## The rewrite you are reading

On 20 July 2026 this repository was reopened under a decision the framework's own logic demanded: work on the governance framework must itself be governed. A modern estate was founded inside this repository, and everything since (including this document) has passed through its gates: planned undertakings, adversarial review of every consequential artefact, deterministic confidentiality sweeps, and the author's approval at every boundary.

That estate then audited the whole lineage. A frozen registry of thirty-two organs (mechanisms and disciplines that could each be independently adopted, traced, or killed) was traced across all three living estates, cell by cell, with commit-level receipts and every verdict made first-hand. The audit corrected the record in both directions. It moved the framework's birth earlier than the canon knew, to June 2025 in the origin repository. And it corrected the canon's own claims about itself, including a promotional "complete system in thirteen days" that the author's honest retrospective revised to a walking skeleton.

The result is this canon: a fresh narrative where none existed, field-hardened documents transplanted with their provenance recorded, and the 2025 canon archived whole as the exhibit of its epoch.

## What the story teaches

1. **Frameworks born in the field survive transplanting.** Every organ in the [registry](ORGAN_REGISTRY.md) traces to a working repository and a felt need. None began as a whiteboard exercise.
2. **Cross-pollination is a method, and it needs a carrier.** The organs moved between estates deliberately, fitted on arrival, with provenance recorded. The lineage even shares its deaths: one estate's founding backlog refuses a mechanism because a sibling had already disproved it.
3. **Dormancy of the artefact is not dormancy of the idea.** The published canon went quiet for months while the framework kept compounding in private. The gap between the two is why this rewrite exists, and why the new canon treats its own claims as claims.
4. **Governance must be falsifiable or it becomes theatre.** The current epoch's whole character is in its kill roll. Read it next: [`KILLED_MECHANISMS.md`](KILLED_MECHANISMS.md).
