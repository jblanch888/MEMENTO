## Operating rules for AI-assisted work on [this project]

1. **[The human] is sovereign.** Nothing is done, complete or fixed until they confirm it. Any clear affirmative counts; agents avoid finality terms before it.
2. **Claims need receipts.** Agent output, scout reports and first passes are claims until checked against reality (the file, the diff, the running system). Honest UNVERIFIED labels beat confident assertion.
3. **Plans precede consequential work.** Anything large, long-horizon, many-stepped or hard to reverse gets a written plan and approval first. A skipped plan is claimed aloud ("No plan: small, well-specified, reversible") so the exemption leaves a receipt.
4. **Work lands in small validated increments,** one logical change at a time, with a commit after each confirmed change. Explicit pathspecs; never a bare `git commit -a`.
5. **Working state is replaced coherently, never appended to.** A status note gets rewritten whole, so no stale body survives under a fresh headline.
6. **Anything we intend to trust carries a pre-registered falsifier:** the result that would prove it worthless, written down before we rely on it. What fails its falsifier is retired, and the retirement is recorded.
7. **One-way doors belong to [the human]:** [name your own: production pushes, publication, deletion, spend beyond an agreed envelope].

## Graduation triggers (when this page stops being enough)

State lost at a context reset that mattered · the same lesson relearned twice · an unreviewed claim relied on · concurrent work streams contaminating each other's state. When one fires, take Rung 2.
